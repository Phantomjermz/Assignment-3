Exploring Sour Patch Kids

Interesting Facts

Origins and History:

Sour Patch Kids were created by Frank Galatolie in the late 1970s under the name "Mars Men." Later, the candy was rebranded as "Sour Patch Kids" by Cadbury and became widely popular.

Flavors:

Sour Patch Kids come in a variety of fruity flavors, including cherry, orange, lemon, and lime. Each candy piece is coated with a sour sugar layer that gives it a tangy kick.

Iconic Shape:

Sour Patch Kids are shaped like small humanoid figures, often described as resembling children or babies. Each piece is soft and chewy.

Production and Ownership:

Initially created by the Canadian company Jaret International, Sour Patch Kids were later acquired by Cadbury, and eventually became part of Mondelez International, a multinational confectionery, food, and beverage conglomerate.

Popularity:

Sour Patch Kids have become a beloved candy, gaining a dedicated fan base worldwide. They are often enjoyed by people of all ages.
